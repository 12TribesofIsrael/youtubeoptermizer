When enabling the "AI-generated content" label on a TikTok upload for the first time (or the first few times for a given account), TikTok auto-routes the post through human review and sets privacy to **"Only me"** during that window. Review takes anywhere from a few minutes to a couple hours.

**Why:** TikTok spot-checks that the disclosure is accurate. Once the account establishes a pattern of correct AI disclosures (typically 2-3 posts), the review step stops triggering for subsequent posts.

**How to apply:**
- On a first-labeled post, **do not manually override "Only me" → "Everyone"**. Wait for the "Content review complete" notification in TikTok Studio. Manually overriding while review is in progress carries shadow-ban risk — if the reviewer finds anything off, TikTok can then throttle distribution harder than if you'd waited.
- Post during **off-peak hours** (overnight / early morning) the first time you turn the label on, so the 30-120 min review happens during low-engagement time instead of burning prime-time momentum.
- Future posts from the same account with the same label will typically skip review after 2-3 correctly-disclosed posts build trust.
- Content about slavery/bondage/race/religion (common on @aibiblegospels_) can compound first-review risk due to keyword sensitivity filters. Expect slower first-post behavior when these topics intersect with first-time AI disclosure.

**Learned from:** 2026-04-20 Clone #2 upload (Deuteronomy 28 / slave ships). First time we enabled the AI-generated content label. Video was auto-set to "Only me" with "Content under review" flag. User manually flipped to Everyone before review completed — outcome was OK but we got lucky. Cold-start was blunted by the delay.
