## Beat Map

### 0:00–0:15 — Cold Open (no narration)
**Goal:** Visceral hook in 15 sec. Pure footage + ambient audio.
**Direction:** Lead with a real moment that signals stakes. Best candidates:
- A road shot of Zay walking, ambient highway audio
- The "drive by support" moment (community arriving)
- Zay mid-prayer ("hey god" / "the prayer" clips)
- Rain / struggle visual

Recommend opening with a road/struggle shot, not a prayer — keeps the cold open *practical* (he is walking), saves the spiritual weight for Beat 1's payoff.

### 0:15–0:30 — Title Card
**Visual:** Brand identity (gold serif on deep navy/black, sparse golden particles).
**Text:** `3000 MILES FOR THE KIDS — PHILADELPHIA → CALIFORNIA`
**Audio:** Single low cinematic tone.

### 0:30–2:00 — Setup: Who & What
**Narration (Daniel voice):** Introduce Isaiah "Humble Zay" Thomas — Philly minister, walking three thousand miles to California, broadcasting every step live on Twitch. Frame the *what* before the *why*.
**Visuals:** Map graphic showing route (Philadelphia → California), establishing road footage, Day 1 / Day 33 / Day 82 milestone overlays, b-roll of Zay walking.
**Tone:** Measured, grounded. Daniel speaks about a real man, not a biblical figure.

### 2:00–4:30 — Beat 1: The Why (Zay's IG reel — the centerpiece)
**Narration setup (~30 sec):** Daniel asks the question every viewer is already asking — *why?* — and hands the answer to Zay directly. Then we go to the IG reel.

**Zay's monologue (the IG reel, ~30–45 sec):** His own words. The school. The kids. "End up in the system or end up dead." The university he's raising money to build.

**Scripture overlay (gold-on-black card, 3 sec hold):** *"Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this: To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction."* — James 1:27

### 4:30–7:00 — Beat 2: The Road
**Narration:** What it takes. Rain, fatigue, miles, isolation. The body breaks down before the spirit does. *"This is the part of the story you can't fake — you cannot stage a thousand hours of livestream from the side of a highway."*
**Visuals:** "rain clip", "struggle", "Naptown Potholes Undefeated", mile-marker clips, the car-accident-and-recovery footage if usable.
**Pacing:** Slower, contemplative. Sells the realness — directly answers the YPP "inauthentic content" rejection.

### 7:00–10:00 — Beat 3: The Community
**Narration:** Strangers started showing up. Aunties bringing meals. Brothers walking stretches with him. Shoes gifted. AirBnBs paid for. Community investment in real time.
*"This is what it looks like when a community decides one of its own is worth backing — not in theory, on a state highway in the Midwest."*

## Vertical→16:9 Treatment for the IG Reel (Beat 1)

The reel is 1080x1920 (9:16). The doc is 1920x1080 (16:9). Three options:

**Option A — Pillar-box with brand sides (RECOMMENDED):** Center the vertical clip in a 16:9 frame. Fill the left/right side bars with the brand identity background — deep navy/black with sparse golden particles, matching the title cards. Optional: faint gold serif overlay on side bars with `FAITH WALK LIVE` watermark or a relevant scripture phrase.
*Pros:* On-brand, no face crop, professional documentary style, viewer attention stays on Zay's face. *Cons:* slight visual inertia (no horizontal motion in side bars).

**Option B — Blurred-fill background:** Center the vertical clip, fill sides with a heavily blurred + darkened version of the same clip. Common social-media-doc technique.
*Pros:* always-on motion, viewer's eye stays in the center. *Cons:* off-brand (doesn't use the gold/cinematic identity), can feel TikTok-y.

**Option C — Center crop to 16:9:** Crop top/bottom of the vertical to fit. Loses the reel's framing entirely — Zay's face may end up positioned wrong.
*Not recommended.* The reel is composed for vertical; cropping breaks his framing.

**My pick:** Option A. CapCut: drop the reel onto the timeline, scale to fit height (which makes it ~540px wide on a 1920px canvas), then place a brand-identity background image as a layer behind it. Side bars become a permanent canvas for the brand.

## Reviewer-eye QA (before publish)

The YPP reviewer will be looking for "inauthentic content." Pre-publish gut check:
- [ ] Real human face on screen for >70% of runtime? (Zay's actual footage)
- [ ] Real ambient audio (street noise, weather, voices) audible under narration?
- [ ] Real third-party humans visible (community support moments)?
- [ ] Original narrative voice (not just AI text-to-speech reading scripture)?
- [ ] Clear connection to a real-world thing (the GoFundMe, the school, faithwalklive.com tracker, the actual livestream)?

If all 5 boxes check, this is the strongest possible answer to the rejection reason. The pivot to school-fundraiser framing strengthens box #5 specifically — there is now a tangible real-world outcome (a building) tied to the doc's CTA.

## Channel-fit consideration

This doc reads more "social-mission documentary" and less "AI Bible Gospels prophecy content." That's intentional and on-strategy:

- For YPP re-review: tangible, real-world, verifiable mission > AI-generated biblical content. This is the strongest possible counter to "inauthentic."
- For channel growth: the doc is the channel's missing "start here" hero video, and a 12-15 min long-form is the long-form pillar [goal.md](../../goal.md#L70) flagged as missing.
- For brand cohesion: Beat 4 explicitly ties Zay's mission to AI Bible Gospels as faith-tech-for-ministers — the parent brand wraps the story without forcing prophecy framing where it doesn't fit.
- For 12 Tribes audience retention: the community in Beat 3 is overwhelmingly Hebrew Israelite (aunties, brothers, "who's ya mama"), so the cultural identity is *shown* even if not *narrated*.
