Thomas batch-produces AI Bible Gospels Shorts and schedules them 1–2 weeks out in advance. One Short per day at a fixed slot (observed: 20:00 UTC). This has been his operating pattern all along, not a reaction to any current event.

**Why:** On 2026-04-22, during the YPP second-appeal window, I saw 15 scheduled Shorts (all on "12 Tribes of Israel" topic) publishing one-per-day Apr 22 → May 6 and recommended trimming the queue to avoid reinforcing YouTube's "mass-produced" finding. Thomas pushed back — this is literally how he's always worked. The schedule isn't new/reactive; pausing it would be the anomaly, not continuing it.

**How to apply:**
- A queue of 10–15 scheduled Shorts spanning 2 weeks is normal — do NOT flag it as suspicious or recommend trimming unless the content itself matches YouTube's inauthentic-content examples (scrolling text, static slideshow + TTS, identical template with only text overlay changed).
- Topic concentration ≠ mass-production. 12 Tribes of Israel is the channel's core thesis — multiple Shorts on the same topic is niche depth, not repetition.
- During YPP review windows, continuing normal cadence is the correct move. Breaking from it to look "clean" signals reactive behavior and can itself be the suspicious pattern.
- Focus content-quality judgments on the individual video (narration present? original script? visual variety?), not on schedule/volume/topic.
