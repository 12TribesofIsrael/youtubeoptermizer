## Lessons learned — Script 1 test run (2026-04-19)

First run of Script 1 through the Modal pipeline surfaced three failure modes. Every subsequent script must be reviewed against this checklist **before hitting Generate Video**.

### 1. The hook gets paraphrased — ALWAYS manually fix scene 1

Modal Claude treats the opening as a paraphrasable "intro" rather than a verbatim hook. On the first run it produced *"They cannot the truth about Edom's identity. Ancient scriptures hold secrets that reshape everything you thought you knew about biblical history."* — grammatically broken AND the viral-trigger phrase was gone.

**Action:** After Generate Scenes completes, open Scene 1 and **paste the exact first paragraph of the script into the narration field**. Replace whatever Modal Claude produced. The hook phrase *"They don't teach…Read [X] slowly"* must appear word-for-word in the audio — it's the phrase driving the share rate.

### 2. The CTA gets dropped entirely — ALWAYS manually fix the final scene

On the first run, the CTA scene was rewritten as *"Discover the complete revelation. These ancient prophecies unlock the hidden identity of nations…"* — no YouTube mention, no "link in bio," no @AIBIBLEGOSPELS. That kills the cross-platform traffic lift.

**Action:** Open the **final scene** after Generate Scenes. Paste the exact last paragraph of the script into the narration field. The words "YouTube," "link in bio," and "AIBIBLEGOSPELS" must all be in the audio.

### 3. Modal Claude generates too many scenes (8 instead of 4)

Scripts written as 4 paragraphs get split into 7-8 scenes at ~15s each = **~2 min total**. That's too long for TikTok's short-form algorithm (completion-rate penalty). Target: **5-6 scenes / 75-90s**.

**Action:** Before Generate Video, delete redundant scenes. Typical cuts:
- Any scene that **explains** a verse (Modal Claude tends to add an "interpretation" scene after each quoted verse). Delete these — the viewer gets the meaning from the next scene or the visuals.
- Any scene that opens with *"Remember…"* or *"Note…"* — these are usually padding that Modal Claude inserted to meet a scene count.

### Pre-render checklist (use for every script in this batch)

Before clicking Generate Video:

- [ ] Scene 1 narration matches the script's opening paragraph word-for-word (especially the "They don't teach…Read [X] slowly" hook)
- [ ] Final scene narration matches the script's closing paragraph word-for-word (must include "YouTube," "link in bio," "AIBIBLEGOSPELS")
- [ ] Total scene count is 5-6 (delete redundant "interpretation" / "remember" scenes)
- [ ] No image prompt contains "Caucasian," "European," "pale," or "light-skinned"
- [ ] No image prompt has quoted scripture text or on-screen words
- [ ] Every human figure is described as "dark-brown to deep-brown skinned" / "melanated" / "Black Hebrew Israelite" with "wool-textured" or "natural afro" hair

### What Modal Claude gets RIGHT (don't over-edit)

From the Script 1 test, these came out great on the first generation — trust them:
- Melanated character rules ("Black Hebrew Israelite," "deeply melanated skin," "natural afro hair with golden crown") — consistent across all 8 scenes
- Scripture quoted verbatim in scripture scenes
- Chiaroscuro lighting ("golden divine light," "candlelight," "shafts of divine light")
- Biblical settings (ancient scrolls, stone chambers, mountain peaks, desert landscapes)
- Camera motion variety (zoom-in, tilt-up, tracking shots, push-ins)

Edit narration aggressively; edit image prompts only if something drifts off-brand.
