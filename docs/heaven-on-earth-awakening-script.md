## SCENE 1: COLD OPEN — ONE MAN, ONE BOOK (0:00–1:15)

[VISUAL: Text fades in — "NORTH PHILADELPHIA, 2026" — gold serif on black. Then cut to: Dusk. A block in North Philly — row homes, concrete steps, corner store down the street. The streetlights flicker. A dark-brown skinned Black man, African American complexion, deep melanated skin, short cropped 4C coiled hair, full dark beard, wearing a dark hoodie — sits alone on the steps. Head down. Hands clasped. The weight of the world on him. Beside him on the step — an old Bible. Worn leather cover. Gold-edged pages. He's been ignoring it. Camera holds on his face — exhaustion, frustration, searching. Then his hand moves. He picks it up. Opens it slowly. The pages crack.]

**NARRATION:**

He didn't plan on opening it.

It had been sitting there. On the shelf. On the table. On the nightstand. Collecting dust. Collecting silence.

But something pulled him.

[VISUAL: Close-up of his fingers on the pages — dark-brown hands against aged parchment. His eyes begin to scan the words. Camera pushes slowly into the open Bible. The text fills the frame.]

Not a voice. Not a sign. Just a weight he couldn't carry anymore — and a book he'd been walking past his whole life.

[VISUAL: His eyes lock onto a verse. His breathing changes. Something shifts. And then — faint at first — the edges of the pages begin to glow. Not fire. Not electricity. Something warmer. Something older. Golden light — soft, alive — rising from the words themselves.]

"My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge."

He read it. And the book answered back.

## SCENE 2: THE GLOW (1:15–2:30)

[VISUAL: The golden glow intensifies — climbing from the pages up through his hands. His dark-brown skin catches the amber light like polished bronze. The glow moves up his arms, across his chest, over his shoulders. He doesn't flinch. He doesn't run. He sits still — and lets it cover him. The light wraps around him like a shield — a full-body aura of divine golden radiance. His face transforms — not physically, but spiritually. The exhaustion is gone. The searching is over. His eyes are clear. Tears on his cheeks catch the golden light.]

**NARRATION:**

"Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path."

The glow did not burn him. It covered him. Like armor. Like a shield. Like the presence of something that had been waiting — waiting for him to open the book.

[VISUAL: Camera circles him slowly — the golden shield of light radiating outward from his body. The concrete steps beneath him seem to warm. The streetlight above him flickers and dies — it's not needed anymore. He is the light source now. The Bible still open in his hands. His lips moving — reading silently. Each word feeding the glow.]

"The LORD is my rock, and my fortress, and my deliverer — my God, my strength, in whom I will trust. My buckler, and the horn of my salvation, and my high tower."

[VISUAL: The glow pulses — once, twice — then sends a ripple of golden light outward across the ground, like a stone dropped in still water. The ripple races down the street and disappears into the distance.]

And the light did not stay with him alone.

## SCENE 3: SHE PICKS IT UP (2:30–3:30)

[VISUAL: Cut to — a kitchen. Different city. A dark-brown skinned Black woman, African American complexion, deep melanated skin, natural 4C tightly coiled hair, standing at the counter. A Bible sits on the kitchen table behind her. She pauses. Turns. Looks at it. Something pulls her — the same invisible pull. She sits down. Opens it. Close-up of her dark-brown hands on the pages. The same golden glow begins — soft, rising from the text. It climbs through her fingers, up her arms, covers her shoulders, wraps around her like a shawl of divine light. Her eyes close. Peace.]

**NARRATION:**

Across the city — she felt it too.

Not a sound. Not a call. But a pull. Ancient. Familiar. Like remembering something she never forgot.

[VISUAL: The golden shield of light surrounds her completely. She opens her eyes — they reflect amber fire. Her lips move:]

"She openeth her mouth with wisdom — and in her tongue is the law of kindness."

[VISUAL: Another golden ripple radiates outward — through the walls, through the windows, racing across rooftops, merging with the first ripple somewhere in the sky above the city. Two lights becoming one.]

She opened the book. And the book opened her.

## SCENE 4: CITY TO CITY (3:30–5:00)

[VISUAL: RAPID MONTAGE — the pace accelerates. Each shot is 3-4 seconds. Every person is a dark-brown skinned Black man or Black woman, African American complexion, deep melanated skin, 4C coiled hair:]

[VISUAL: Atlanta — a dark-brown skinned Black man in a barber chair. The barber pauses. The man pulls a Bible from his jacket. Opens it. Golden glow erupts. The whole barbershop floods with amber light.]

[VISUAL: New York — a dark-brown skinned Black woman on a subway platform. Bible in her bag. She pulls it out. Opens it against the rumble of the train. The glow rises. Passengers around her — all dark-brown skinned Black men and women — turn to look.]

[VISUAL: Houston — a dark-brown skinned Black man at a construction site. Lunch break. Hard hat off. Bible on his knee. The glow covers him like golden armor. His coworker — another dark-brown skinned Black man — sees it, reaches into his own bag, pulls out a Bible.]

[VISUAL: Chicago — a dark-brown skinned Black mother reading to her children at bedtime. The Bible open on the bed. The golden glow covers all of them — a dome of light over the bed. The children's 4C coiled hair catches golden light like halos.]

[VISUAL: London — a dark-brown skinned Black man in a winter coat, standing at a bus stop in the rain. He opens a Bible. The rain around him turns golden. Each droplet catches the light.]

[VISUAL: Jamaica — a dark-brown skinned Black woman on a porch, bare feet, Bible on her lap. The glow covers her. The entire yard turns amber.]

**NARRATION:**

"Arise, shine — for thy light is come, and the glory of the LORD is risen upon thee."

One opened the book. Then another. Then another.

Not organized. Not planned. Not a movement built by man.

Something was moving. Something ancient. Something that had been sealed — now unsealed. Something written for this exact generation.

[VISUAL: Aerial shot — city after city lighting up. Golden domes of light appearing like candles being lit. Atlanta. New York. Houston. Chicago. London. Jamaica. The lights multiply faster — Philadelphia, Detroit, Oakland, Birmingham, Miami, Toronto, Lagos, Accra, Kingston, São Paulo —]

"For the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea."

The darkness had no strategy for this. Because the light was not coming from a pulpit. It was coming from the pages. One person at a time.

## SCENE 5: THE GLOBE (5:00–6:30)

[VISUAL: Camera begins to rise — pulling back from the streets, above the rooftops, above the skyline. Higher. Higher. The golden points of light multiply below — hundreds, then thousands, then millions. Camera continues pulling back — through clouds, through the atmosphere. The curvature of the earth appears. And now we see it — the entire globe. Dark on one side, dawn breaking on the other. And scattered across every continent — golden lights. Flickering. Growing. Connecting.]

**NARRATION:**

Now pull back. See what the Most High sees.

[VISUAL: The golden lights begin to connect — lines of golden energy running between them like a web, like a circuit, like veins carrying blood. North America is lit up. South America is lit up. The Caribbean glows bright — almost solid gold. Africa's western coast pulses. London. Paris. Amsterdam — wherever the scattered children of Israel are, the light finds them.]

Every city. Every nation. Every forgotten corner where the children of Israel were scattered — the book is being opened.

[VISUAL: The web of golden light now covers the globe like a net. But now — new lights appear in places beyond the diaspora. A white man in rural Appalachia opens a Bible on his porch — the glow covers him. An Asian woman in Tokyo opens the book on a park bench — the glow covers her. A Latino family in Mexico City gathers around a kitchen table Bible — the glow covers them all. An Indigenous man in the Australian outback reads under the stars — the glow covers him. The light does not ask where you come from. It asks if you will open the book.]

[VISUAL: The earth rotates slowly. The original golden lights from the Israelite awakening burn brightest — they started it. But now every continent pulses with amber radiance as ALL nations join. The darkness between the lights shrinks — the glow expanding, merging, becoming one unified field of golden light wrapping the earth.]

Israel woke up first. But the book was never for one people alone. It was written so that ALL nations would see the light — and walk in it.

Not by might. Not by power. But by His spirit.

[VISUAL: A single massive beam of golden light descends from above — from beyond the frame, from beyond the stars — and touches the top of the globe. The light spreads across the surface like sunrise, but from above. The beam widens. The whole earth is bathed in divine golden radiance. The globe is no longer dark. It is covered.]

And the beam that touches the earth — does not come from the earth.

It comes from above.

## SCENE 6: HEAVEN ON EARTH (6:30–8:00)

[VISUAL: Camera descends slowly back toward the earth — through the golden beam, through the clouds now glowing amber, back down to the streets. But the streets are different now. The same North Philly block from Scene 1 — but the concrete is warm with golden light. The streetlights are off. They're not needed. The golden glow is everywhere — in the walls, in the ground, in the air itself. People walk the streets — dark-brown skinned Black men and women leading the way, Bibles in hand, light in their eyes. And now among them — people of every nation. White, Asian, Latino, every complexion — all carrying Bibles, all covered in the same golden glow. No separation. No division. Children of every background laughing together. Elders smiling. Families together. No fear. No weight. No chains. Israel started the fire — and now the whole world is warm.]

**NARRATION:**

"And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying — Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God."

[VISUAL: Back to North Philadelphia. The first man from Scene 1 stands up from the concrete steps. Bible still in his hands. The golden shield still around him. But now — he's not alone. Hundreds of people walk the North Philly streets around him, each carrying their own glow. He looks around. He sees them. They see him. A slow smile crosses his face — dark-brown skin catching golden light, tears still drying on his cheeks.]

This is not the end of the world. This is the beginning.

[VISUAL: The woman from Scene 3 walks toward him through the crowd — golden light around her, Bible in her hands. They stand face to face. No words. Just recognition. They know what happened. They know what's happening. The camera slowly pulls back as more people gather — a growing congregation of light-bearers. The golden aura from each person merges until the entire street is one solid field of divine radiance.]

Heaven is not a place you go when you die.

It is what happens when a people open the book — and the book opens them.

Israel opened it first. And the nations followed.

[VISUAL: Final wide shot — the street from above. A river of golden light flowing through the city. Then higher — the city from above. Then higher — the nation. Then higher — the globe, fully illuminated, rotating slowly in the golden beam from heaven. The earth glows like a sun.]

[VISUAL: Screen holds on the glowing earth for three full seconds. Then slow fade to warm black.]

[VISUAL: Three scripture cards appear one at a time, gold serif on black, each holding for three seconds:]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Arise, shine; for thy light is come, and the glory of the LORD is risen upon thee." — Isaiah 60:1]

Shalom.
